The
Trenches:
A Soundscape

World War I trenches, as seen on the Western Front, were a complex network of deep, zigzagging ditches designed for defensive warfare. Consisting of front-line, support, and reserve trenches, soldiers endured harsh conditions, including flooding, mud, and unsanitary environments. Barbed wire, sandbags, and obstacles were used as additional layers of defense, and the land between opposing lines, or No Man's Land posed constant danger. The constant threat of enemy attacks, artillery shelling, and gas assaults, alongside static nature of trench warfare contributed to thousands of casualites and very little gain of land, the Battle of the Somme being a prominent example.
